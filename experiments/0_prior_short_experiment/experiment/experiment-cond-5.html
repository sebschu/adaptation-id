Computational Linguistics at Saarland University

In this experiment, you will see a series of visual scenes and will be asked to answer some questions about them. The experiment should take ~5 minutes. Please pay attention, thanks!

LEGAL INFORMATION:
This experiment is being conducted as part of ongoing research at Saarland University. If you have any questions or comments about the study, please contact us on Prolific. You must be at least 18 years old to participate. Your participation in this research is voluntary. There are no risks or benefits to participating in this study. Instructions

On the following pages, you will see a scene with a man and a girl standing next to a table with a gumball machine on it. Importantly, the girl cannot see the gumball machine as it is too high up for her to see. The man can see all the gumballs in the machine, which are constantly being tossed around.

After observing each scene, consider the two sentences below the scene and rate how likely you think it is that the man would say each of the two sentences. If you think that both answers are unlikely, you can indicate this by giving the third option, something else, a high rating. To rate the sentences, use the sliders to distribute 100 points across the two sentences and something else. The sliders will automatically snap back if you try to assign more than 100 points.
